Recipe

  1. Boil the potatoes (peeled or washed with the skin) in a pot with cold water and salt. Once cooked, remove from the pot, run through cold water, let it cool (peel if they were cooked with the skin on) and make a smooth puree.
  2. Season with salt and pepper, season with nutmeg and add the flour in a sprinkle, the beaten egg (if it is small, use 2) and form a homogeneous dough. Cool for 10 minutes in the refrigerator.
  3. Take small portions of dough and make long, thin rolls to shape the gnocchi.
  4. Arrange on a clean, floured surface. Cut gnocchi approximately 2.5 cm long, leaving them on the floured table so that they do not stick. Then, pass them through the tines of a fork or a fork, applying slight pressure with your thumb to shape them.
  5. Boil water in a large pot with a handful of coarse salt. Carefully add the gnocchi and as they rise to the surface they will be ready to remove with the slotted spoon.
  6. Serve directly on each plate and add the chosen sauce.
